Vladimir Putin

President of Russia

Early Life

Vladimir Vladimirovich Putin was born on October 7, 1952, in Leningrad (now St. Petersburg), Russia. He grew up in a working-class family and developed an interest in martial arts and the KGB (Committee for State Security) during his youth.

Education and Early Career

Putin graduated from Leningrad State University in 1975 with a degree in law. He then joined the KGB, where he served as an intelligence officer for many years. His time in the KGB helped shape his political career and strategy in later years.

Political Career

Putin's political rise began when he joined the administration of St. Petersburg in the 1990s. He became head of the Federal Security Service (FSB) in 1998 and was appointed Prime Minister in 1999. After Boris Yeltsin’s resignation, Putin became acting president and was elected president in 2000. He has served multiple terms as president and prime minister of Russia.

Presidency

Putin’s presidency has been marked by significant economic growth, but also by controversies regarding human rights, media freedoms, and Russia’s foreign policy. He has sought to strengthen Russia's influence on the global stage, particularly in Europe and the Middle East.

Accomplishments

  • Re-established Russia as a major global power following the collapse of the Soviet Union.
  • Oversaw significant economic growth, largely driven by oil and gas exports.
  • Centralized power and strengthened the role of the Russian government in daily life.
  • Influenced global politics, particularly through the annexation of Crimea in 2014 and involvement in Syria.
  • Introduced policies that have fostered a more controlled media landscape in Russia.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1952: Born in Leningrad, Russia.
  • 1975: Graduated from Leningrad State University and joined the KGB.
  • 1999: Appointed Prime Minister of Russia.
  • 2000: Became President of Russia after Boris Yeltsin’s resignation.
  • 2014: Annexed Crimea, escalating tensions with Ukraine and the West.
  • 2020: Signed a law allowing him to potentially remain in power until 2036.
