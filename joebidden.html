Joe Biden

46th President of the United States

Early Life

Joseph Robinette Biden Jr. was born on November 20, 1942, in Scranton, Pennsylvania. He grew up in a working-class family and moved to Delaware as a child. Biden displayed an early interest in leadership and public service.

Education and Legal Career

Biden attended the University of Delaware, where he majored in history and political science. He later earned his law degree from Syracuse University. After completing his education, he began practicing law and entered local politics in Delaware.

Political Career

Joe Biden was first elected to the U.S. Senate in 1972, becoming one of the youngest senators in American history. He served as a senator from Delaware for over 30 years. In 2009, he became vice president under President Barack Obama, serving two terms. In 2020, Biden ran for president and won, becoming the 46th president of the United States.

Presidency

Biden's presidency has been focused on addressing the COVID-19 pandemic, advancing infrastructure projects, and promoting social and economic equity. His leadership emphasizes bipartisanship and restoring America's global alliances.

Accomplishments

  • Served as vice president for two terms under Barack Obama.
  • Authored key legislative measures, including the Violence Against Women Act.
  • Managed major COVID-19 vaccination rollouts during his presidency.
  • Focused on infrastructure investment with the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
  • Reestablished international cooperation on climate change by rejoining the Paris Agreement.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1942: Born in Scranton, Pennsylvania.
  • 1972: Elected to the U.S. Senate at the age of 29.
  • 2009: Became vice president of the United States under Barack Obama.
  • 2020: Elected as the 46th president of the United States.
  • 2021: Sworn in as president and began initiatives to combat COVID-19 and rebuild infrastructure.
