George Washington

The First President of the United States

Early Life

George Washington was born on February 22, 1732, in Westmoreland County, Virginia. He grew up on a plantation, and early on, he showed an interest in surveying, which led him to become an expert in land surveying.

Military Career

Washington's military career began during the French and Indian War, where he gained valuable experience and leadership skills. His performance in battle made him a prominent figure in colonial Virginia.

Presidency

In 1789, Washington was elected as the first president of the United States. He served two terms from 1789 to 1797, setting many precedents for the presidency, including the formation of the first cabinet and establishing the practice of serving only two terms.

Accomplishments

  • Led the Continental Army to victory during the American Revolutionary War
  • Presided over the Constitutional Convention of 1787
  • Founded the nation's capital, Washington, D.C.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1732: Born in Westmoreland County, Virginia.
  • 1754-1763: Served in the French and Indian War.
  • 1787: Presided over the Constitutional Convention.
  • 1789: Elected as the first President of the United States.
  • 1797: Retired after two terms as President.
